Our Programs

Transformative, high-impact, and youth-centered initiatives addressing real challenges facing Kenyan youth and communities.

Programs Overview

Youth Synergy Network implements transformative, high-impact, and youth-centered programs anchored on five thematic pillars: Legal Literacy, Mental Health, Environmental Justice, Mentorship, and Sanitary Dignity. These programs are designed to respond to real challenges facing Kenyan youth and communities, especially in marginalized and under-resourced areas.

Every program is youth-led, data-informed, and results-driven — built to equip the next generation with knowledge, confidence, and agency.

⚖️

Legal Literacy Program – "Know Your Rights" Initiative

Goal: To equip young people and the general public with practical knowledge of the law, human rights, and constitutional processes.

Core Activities:

  • Legal clinics in communities and schools
  • Constitution simplified workshops
  • Mock trials and moot courts
  • Street law campaigns (e.g., "Law on the Go" caravan)
  • Pocket Constitution distribution
  • Community barazas on land rights, criminal justice, gender laws

Impact Metrics:

3,200+

Youth trained in constitutional rights

75+

Legal awareness forums across 8 counties

200+

Paralegal youth certified

Mental Health Program – "Healing Circles"

Goal: To create safe, supportive, and stigma-free environments for youth to talk about mental health, access support, and express themselves through creativity.

Core Activities:

  • Campus and high school wellness clubs
  • Peer support groups and "healing circles"
  • Art therapy, poetry therapy, and dance therapy events
  • Mental health awareness weeks
  • Suicide prevention and crisis response training
  • Partnerships with licensed counselors and psychologists

Impact Metrics:

30+

Active wellness clubs

5,000+

Youth reached through forums

60+

Mental health ambassadors trained

🌱

Environmental Justice Program – "Green Justice Movement"

Goal: To empower young people to become environmental defenders, climate advocates, and agents of ecological restoration.

Core Activities:

  • Tree planting campaigns (1 million trees by 2030 target)
  • Climate dialogues in campuses and public spaces
  • Clean-up drives and waste awareness workshops
  • Community-based climate data collection
  • Environmental law education
  • Green leadership training and policy hackathons

Impact Metrics:

18,000+

Trees planted in 12 counties

60+

Green events conducted

✓

Mentorship & Academic Empowerment Program – "Rise & Lead"

Goal: To help students unlock their academic and leadership potential through peer mentorship, public speaking, and career exposure.

Core Activities:

  • University-to-high school mentorship days
  • Academic motivation rallies
  • Reading culture revival programs
  • Leadership bootcamps and forums
  • Talent and innovation exhibitions
  • Law career days and CV writing sessions

Impact Metrics:

130+

Secondary schools reached

25,000+

Students mentored nationwide

✓

Youth Mentorship Toolkit created

👧

Sanitary Dignity Campaign – "Pads for Power"

Goal: To end period poverty through the free distribution of pads, menstrual health education, and community advocacy for dignity.

Core Activities:

  • Sanitary pad mobilization drives in campuses and churches
  • Menstrual health forums in schools
  • Creation of Menstrual Ambassadors from among student leaders
  • Community barazas on de-stigmatizing menstruation
  • Mapping schools with urgent menstrual needs
  • Advocacy for county and national government support

Impact Metrics:

70+

4

Cross-Cutting Program Strategies

Our approach to implementing all YSN programs follows these key principles:

Youth-Led Implementation

Programs designed and executed by student and youth volunteers

Research & Data

All activities are tracked, documented, and evaluated for outcomes

Community Inclusion

Programs co-created with local leaders, school heads, and county officials

Digital Integration

Campaigns amplified through social media, podcasts, and e-learning

Sustainability

Gradual transfer of program leadership to local youth clubs, especially in schools

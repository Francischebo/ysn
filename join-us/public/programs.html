Our Transformative Programs

Discover how Youth Synergy Network is empowering young people through action-oriented initiatives across Kenya.

Our Approach

The Youth Synergy Network (YSN) runs transformative programs built on five interconnected pillars of action: Legal Empowerment, Mental Health, Climate Justice, Gender Dignity, and Youth Mentorship. Each program is strategically developed to address real challenges affecting young people across Kenya, using a grassroots-led, rights-based, and solution-oriented approach.

⚖️

Youth Empowerment & Legal Awareness

This flagship initiative seeks to equip young people — especially university and high school students — with practical knowledge of the law, civic rights, leadership ethics, and policy-making processes.

Key Components:

  • Youth Legal Literacy Caravan: A mobile program that tours counties holding interactive forums, mock trials, and legal mentorships
  • Know Your Rights Campaigns: Workshops on constitutional rights, sexual & reproductive health rights, labor law, and access to justice
  • Youth & the Constitution Forums: Legal literacy discussions based on the 2010 Constitution of Kenya with law experts and civic educators

Outcomes So Far:

Climate Change & Environmental Justice

Recognizing that the climate crisis is a youth crisis, this campaign empowers young people to be front-line defenders of their environments. Activities combine climate education, sustainability training, and grassroots eco-action.

Key Components:

  • Tree Planting Drives: In collaboration with schools and county governments
  • Green Clubs in Schools: Youth-led clubs focused on environmental preservation
  • Climate Justice Dialogues: Campus-based conversations linking climate change to inequality, land rights, and policy gaps
  • Sustainable Living Trainings: On clean energy, waste management, water conservation

Impact Highlights:

400+

Trees planted across five counties

15

Schools established green clubs

8

Public climate forums hosted

Mental Health Advocacy

Addressing Kenya's mental health crisis — especially among youth — this program creates safe spaces for peer support, trauma healing, and mental wellness conversations.

Key Components:

  • Mental Health Dialogues: Interactive sessions facilitated by trained psychologists and peer counselors
  • Campus Wellness Clubs: Run by trained peer leaders, offering counseling referrals and support networks
  • Art for Healing Events: Using spoken word, visual art, and music to express emotion and share healing journeys
  • Mental Health Policy Advocacy: Campaigning for better mental health infrastructure in schools

Achievements:

10+

Mental health clubs launched

30+

Forums facilitated

100+

Youth referred to professional care

👧

Provision of Sanitary Pads: The Dignity Drive

YSN champions menstrual dignity as a human right. Through the Sanitary Pads for Dignity Initiative, the Network donates sanitary products to girls in marginalized schools while conducting menstrual hygiene education.

Key Components:

  • Pad Drives in Rural Schools
  • Menstrual Health Workshops (run by nurses and trained educators)
  • Boys for Dignity Sessions (engaging male students in empathy-based conversations)
  • Policy Dialogue with Counties to integrate menstrual products into budgets

Results to Date:

8,000+

Pads distributed

25

Schools reached

100+

Boys trained as "Dignity Ambassadors"

Mentorship & High School Motivation Tours

This program bridges the inspiration gap by connecting university student leaders and professionals with high school students across Kenya. It promotes academic discipline, goal-setting, leadership, and career direction.

Key Components:

  • Campus-to-Classroom Tours: Law students and alumni visit schools to mentor
  • Vision Boards & Life Skills Sessions
  • Law as a Career Workshops: With law students and advocates
  • Girls in Law Campaign: Encouraging high-performing girls to pursue law and leadership roles

Achievements:

50+

Mentorship visits across counties

1,500+

Students mentored

Increase in law school applicants

Women & Youth in Governance Forums (NEW in 2025)

A program dedicated to equipping young women and youth from marginalized communities with leadership skills, public speaking ability, and civic confidence to engage in decision-making.

Focus Areas:

  • Leadership training for young women
  • Public speaking and civic engagement workshops
  • Decision-making skills for youth from marginalized communities
  • Engagement with county and national governance structures

Program Status:

This exciting new initiative is launching in 2025. Join us as we empower the next generation of women and youth leaders in governance!

University Chapters

The heartbeat of our grassroots leadership model, empowering students across Kenya to become agents of change

Overview

The University Chapters of Youth Synergy Network represent the heartbeat of our grassroots leadership model. These are semi-autonomous, student-run branches established in Kenyan universities offering law degrees, operating under the broader mission, constitution, and vision of YSN.

Each chapter serves as a local hub for legal empowerment, climate action, mentorship, gender dignity campaigns, and mental health advocacy. The chapters embody YSN's belief that youth leadership must be decentralized, democratic, and context-responsive.

Spearhead peer mentorship in surrounding high schools and communities

Foundational Principles

The core values and frameworks that guide our chapters

  • Each chapter aligns with YSN's five pillars: Legal Literacy, Mental Health, Climate Action, Mentorship, and Sanitary Dignity.
  • Chapters operate under the guidance of a National Secretariat and follow a standardized Constitution and Chapter Policy Manual.
  • Collaboration across chapters is facilitated through annual leadership summits, regional forums, and inter-campus dialogues.

Liaison Officer

Media & Publicity Director

Manages digital platforms and event visibility

How to Join or Start a Chapter

YSN welcomes youth from other institutions to apply to start a new chapter

Requirements to Qualify

  • The university must offer a law degree
  • At least 15 founding members needed
  • Must commit to a one-year activity calendar
  • Formal application submitted to the National Secretariat
Download Application Form

Benefits After Approval

  • Branding materials (banners, shirts, brochures)
  • Access to national forums and grants
  • Training and mentorship from existing leaders
  • Support for campus events and outreach
  • Networking opportunities with legal professionals
